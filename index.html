If a stock’s price rises sharply in a very short time, it often has a high probability of falling again.

Sometimes, a stock’s price suddenly rises or falls sharply within a short time. For example, a stock priced at ₹100 may jump to ₹105 in just 1–5 minutes. Often, there is a high chance that the price will quickly drop back down. Intraday traders take advantage of this by short selling at ₹105 and exiting the trade when the price falls back to ₹100–₹102, booking a profit. This type of trading, done against the prevailing trend, is known as Spike Reversal or Exhaustion Spike.
